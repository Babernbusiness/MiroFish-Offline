# Is Trump's pause on attacking Iranian energy for diplomacy or an escalation?

**Source:** BBC News
**Author:** James Landale, Diplomatic Correspondent

Donald Trump's decision to pause any attack on Iranian energy plants for a further 10 days could be a pivotal moment in a conflict that has now lasted almost four weeks.

The US president's commitment to deadlines is fluid — this is his second extension of this particular threat — but he uses them nonetheless for a purpose: to send signals, to distract attention and to buy time.

Trump held off a threatened "obliteration" of Iran's energy infrastructure, a massive escalation that could trigger both Iranian retaliation against similar Gulf facilities and damage chances of a sustainable peace and global economic recovery.

The latest pause was announced minutes after trading closed on Wall Street — widely seen as an attempt to calm international markets. The president pointed to positive signs about the prospects of a diplomatic solution.

## Diplomacy

Messages are being exchanged between the US and Iran via intermediaries, especially Pakistan. Both sides are largely issuing maximalist, irreconcilable demands, but there is a suggestion of a possible meeting in Pakistan.

Diplomats are sceptical. "There is a lot of smoke and mirrors," one said. "There is scepticism that a trusted channel of communication that can bear some load will emerge." Trump insists talks are taking place and going well.

## Military Buildup

The pause also gives the US time to prepare for a potential attack:

- An expeditionary force of ~2,000 US Marines is en route to the Middle East from Japan
- Several thousand US paratroopers are heading to the region from California
- The Pentagon declined to comment on a Wall Street Journal report that another 10,000 troops could be sent

Trump on Thursday: *"If they don't [do a deal], we're their worse nightmare. We'll just keep blowing them away."*

## Status Quo

In the short term, the pause reinforces the current situation:

- US and Israeli attacks on Iranian military targets continue
- Iranian retaliation continues
- The **Strait of Hormuz** remains closed to most merchant traffic

A war that began amid talk of regime change and demilitarising Iran has largely become a question of who controls a narrow stretch of water on which the global economy depends. The deadline extension effectively allows Iran to maintain its restrictions on the strait for another ten days.

## Key Questions

- Is Trump giving himself military options for a ground invasion of strategic locations in Iran?
- Is the military buildup a negotiating threat to force Iran into a deal?
- Can a diplomatic channel through Pakistan produce a sustainable agreement?
- How long can the Strait of Hormuz remain closed before lasting economic damage sets in?
